Since I was a kid I've been interested in video games, not only on how good they are, or if they are fun to play, but also on how they work. For most of my life I focused more on the design, on what makes games tick, and why are people so enthralled by them. I started to ask myself questions about this while I played, and the answers that I couldn't answer by myself I searched online, and that only opened more doors to an ocean of new information.

It was only a couple of years ago that I asked myself the question: "What if I make a game?". This simple question led me to get lost in the rabbit hole that is game development. When I had to decide on what career I wanted to pursue, I chose the road that most of my heroes in the game development world chose, computer science.

Now, as a UIC computer science student, I use what I learn in class to apply it to video game development, and I've recently started to work on my own project.

Skills: C++, Python, Unity, Godot
